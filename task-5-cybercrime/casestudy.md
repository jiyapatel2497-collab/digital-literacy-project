Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details. This is usually done by sending fake emails, messages, or links that appear to be from trusted sources like banks or popular websites.

A typical phishing attack begins when a user receives a message claiming that there is an urgent issue with their account, such as “Your bank account will be blocked. Click here to verify.” The message often creates a sense of urgency to make the user act quickly without thinking. When the user clicks on the link, they are taken to a fake website that looks very similar to the original one. The user then enters their login details or OTP, which are immediately captured by the attacker.

Students and young internet users are often targeted because they may not always recognize such scams. The consequences can be serious, including loss of money, identity theft, or unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while clicking on links and sharing personal information online.
